# ClosetAI - Go-To-Market Strategy

## TL;DR

Launch as a PWA targeting women 22-40 in Mumbai/Bangalore/Delhi. Grow through Instagram content + micro-influencer partnerships + product-led growth (sharing outfit screenshots). Hit 1,000 users in month 1, 10,000 by month 6.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-8)

### Build the Waitlist

**Landing Page (closetai.app)**
- Single page: headline, 3 value props, demo video/GIF, email capture
- Headline: "Your closet, but smarter."
- Subhead: "AI-powered outfit suggestions from YOUR actual wardrobe. Stop staring at a full closet saying 'I have nothing to wear.'"
- CTA: "Join the waitlist ‚Äî first 500 get Pro free for 3 months"

**Waitlist Mechanics:**
- Email capture with referral program
- "Move up the list by sharing" ‚Äî each referral = +10 positions
- Weekly email updates showing product progress (build in public)
- Target: 500 emails before launch

### Content Seeding

**Instagram Account (@closetai.app)**
- Start posting 3-4 weeks before launch
- Content pillars:
  1. **"Did you know?"** ‚Äî wardrobe statistics (e.g., "Average woman wears only 30% of her closet")
  2. **"Outfit math"** ‚Äî show how 10 items = 30+ outfits with the right combinations
  3. **"Closet confessions"** ‚Äî relatable memes about "nothing to wear"
  4. **Product teasers** ‚Äî sneak peeks of the app UI, features
  5. **Style tips** ‚Äî quick styling advice that positions us as experts

**Target:** 1,000 followers before launch

### Micro-Influencer Outreach (20-30 people)

**Who:**
- Fashion micro-influencers (5K-50K followers) in India
- Not mega-celebrities ‚Äî real women who talk about daily styling
- Focus: Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi (our target cities)
- Mix: working professionals who share OOTD, fashion students, style bloggers

**Offer:**
- Free Premium account for 6 months
- Early access to app before public launch
- "Founding Influencer" badge in app
- Commission on referrals (10% of Pro subscriptions from their unique link)

**Ask:**
- 1 Instagram Story showing them using the app
- 1 feed post/reel about their experience
- Honest feedback (we want real reactions, not scripted ads)

**Budget:** $0 (product-only compensation)

## Phase 3: Growth Loops (Months 2-6)

### Growth Loop 1: Outfit Sharing (Viral)

```
User gets outfit suggestion
    ‚Üí Taps "Share" ‚Üí generates beautiful outfit card image
    ‚Üí Posts to Instagram Story / WhatsApp status
    ‚Üí Card includes "Made with ClosetAI" watermark + link
    ‚Üí Friends see ‚Üí curious ‚Üí visit closetai.app ‚Üí sign up
```

**Key design:** The share image must be BEAUTIFUL. Instagram-worthy. Not a screenshot ‚Äî a designed card with:
- Outfit items arranged aesthetically
- Style score badge
- User's Style DNA archetype
- "closetai.app" subtle branding

**Viral coefficient target:** 0.3 (every 10 users bring 3 new ones)

### Growth Loop 2: Closet Challenge (Social)

```
"The 30-Day Closet Challenge"
    ‚Üí Use only items in your closet for 30 days (no new purchases)
    ‚Üí ClosetAI generates daily outfits from existing wardrobe
    ‚Üí Track and share progress
    ‚Üí Community leaderboard
    ‚Üí At end: "You saved ‚ÇπX by not impulse-buying"
```

**Positioning:** Sustainability + style + savings = triple win
**Launch timing:** January (New Year's resolution) or before major sales (anti-sale campaign)

### Growth Loop 3: Referral Program

```
Invite a friend ‚Üí both get 1 month of Pro free
    ‚Üí Friend signs up and uploads 10+ items
    ‚Üí Both rewarded
    ‚Üí Viral: each Pro trial converts at 20% to paid
```

**Economics:** Cost of 1 free Pro month = ~$0.14 (AI cost only). If 20% convert = $1 revenue for $0.14 spend = 7:1 ROI

### Growth Loop 4: Content Marketing (SEO + Social)

**Blog (closetai.app/blog)**
- Weekly articles targeting search intent:
  - "How to build a capsule wardrobe for Indian weather"
  - "Best outfit combinations for 10 basic items"
  - "What colors suit warm skin tones?"
  - "How to dress for Indian office culture"
- Each article includes CTA to try ClosetAI

**YouTube/Reels (monthly)**
- "I let AI pick my outfits for a week" ‚Äî creator collaboration
- Before/after wardrobe organization
- "Closet audit" ‚Äî analyzing a real person's wardrobe with the app

### Daily Engagement Drivers

1. **Morning notification:** "Your outfit for today is ready ‚òÄÔ∏è" (8 AM)
   - Personalized, includes weather, shows outfit preview
   - Open rate target: 30%+

2. **Streak system:** "7-day outfit streak! üî•"
   - Consecutive days of accepting outfit suggestions
   - Achievements: 7-day, 30-day, 100-day streaks
   - Shareable milestone cards

3. **Weekly digest:** "This week in your closet" (Sunday)
   - Items worn / not worn
   - Most versatile item
   - Suggestion for next week

### Monthly Engagement

4. **Seasonal wardrobe review** (quarterly)
   - "Monsoon is coming ‚Äî let's review your rain-ready items"
   - Prompts to add/archive seasonal items

5. **Style DNA evolution**
   - Monthly update: "Your style has shifted toward [more casual]"
   - Comparison with previous months
   - Celebrate positive changes

### Reducing Churn

| Churn Signal | Intervention |
|-------------|-------------|
| No login for 3 days | Push: "Miss us? Here's today's outfit suggestion" |
| No items uploaded for 14 days | Push: "Your closet is waiting ‚Äî add your latest purchase!" |
| Rejected 5 outfits in a row | In-app: "Outfits not hitting? Let's update your style preferences" |
| Approaching item limit (free) | In-app: "You have 28/30 items. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited" |
| Pro user, low engagement | Email: personal note from founder, ask what's not working |

## Target Cities (India Launch)

### Tier 1: Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi-NCR
- Highest concentration of target demographic
- Fashion-forward, tech-savvy
- High smartphone penetration
- Multiple dress code contexts (office + casual + ethnic + party)

### Tier 2 (Month 3+): Hyderabad, Pune, Chennai, Kolkata
- Growing professional class
- Active Instagram fashion communities
- Lower competition for attention

### Why India First (Not US)
1. **Underserved market:** No dominant wardrobe AI app in India
2. **Complex wardrobes:** Western + ethnic + fusion = MORE value from AI
3. **Lower CAC:** Influencer marketing costs 5-10x less than US
4. **Growing market:** India's fashion e-commerce growing 20%+ YoY
5. **Test & learn:** cheaper to iterate before US expansion
6. **Cultural expertise:** founders are Indian, understand the context

## Competitive Moat Over Time

```
Month 1:  Product quality (AI accuracy, beautiful UI)
Month 3:  User data (wardrobe photos, style preferences)
Month 6:  Behavioral data (wear patterns, accept/reject signals)
Month 9:  Network effects (community, shared outfits)

```
